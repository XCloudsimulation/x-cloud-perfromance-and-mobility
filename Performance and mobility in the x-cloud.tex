\begin{abstract}
%\boldmath
In a \xcloud{} topology the cloud resources are geographically dispersed throughout the mobile network. Services are actively located with close proximity to the \ue. Geographically migrating a service between \dcs{}, following a \ues{} movement, imposes a load on the involved \dcs. Shifting loads as a result of random \ue{} movement presents a fundamental problem to the \xcloud{} paradigm.

This paper determines the fundamental service performance issues in a system of mobile users with dispersed \dcs{}, in relation to the placement of the \dcs{} and explores the \ue{} utility of subscribing to \dc{} at a certain network depth.

\end{abstract}
